Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein, born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, was a theoretical physicist renowned for developing the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). His mass-energy equivalence formula E=mc², which arises from the theory of special relativity, is one of the most famous equations in the world. Einstein's work led to significant advancements in our understanding of space, time, and gravitation, fundamentally altering the field of physics. He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, a pivotal step in the development of quantum theory. Besides his scientific achievements, Einstein was known for his passionate advocacy for civil rights, pacifism, and education. He passed away on April 18, 1955, in Princeton, New Jersey, but remains one of the most celebrated scientists in history, synonymous with genius and creativity.

  • Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, into a secular Jewish family.
  • As a child, he was deeply curious and particularly fascinated by a compass shown by his father, which sparked his interest in sciences.
  • Contrary to popular myths, Einstein excelled academically.
  • He was, however, dissatisfied with the rigid teaching methods of his schools.
  • Einstein's family moved to Italy when he was a teenager.
  • This move led him to leave formal education early and renounce his German citizenship.
  • Albert Einstein began his formal education in physics at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich (now ETH Zurich) in 1896.
  • Studied to become a teacher in physics and mathematics.
  • Preferred self-study over attending classes.
  • Graduated in 1900.
  • Initially struggled to secure a teaching position after graduation.
  • Worked as a technical assistant in the Swiss Patent Office in Bern.
  • Developed some of his most significant theories during his time at the Patent Office.
  • Albert Einstein was a pivotal physicist in his later years.
  • Known for his theories of relativity and the equation E=mc².
  • Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.
  • Made significant contributions to quantum theory and other scientific areas.
  • Fled Nazi Germany and settled in the United States.
  • Worked at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton until his death in 1955.
  • Vocal advocate for civil rights and pacifism.
  • Left a lasting legacy in both academia and social activism.
